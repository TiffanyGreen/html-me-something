FLOATING

Floating, or Sensory Deprivation, is the practice of allowing your mind and body to rest in a low-sensory environment. You lay in what is essentially a big bathtub that has around 1,000 lbs of epsom salt dissolved into 10.5 inches of water. When you lay in that salty-water solution, your body immediately floats to the surface, supported in every way. The water sits at a temperature where your skin doesn't recognize heat (93.5 degrees). It's completely dark, and completely silent. What your body and mind can do to rejuvenate in 90 minutes of "sensory deprivation" is truly incredible.

Benefits of Floating

Floating is known to have MANY benefits. Most noteworthy are: increased focus, expanded creativity, reduction of muscle soreness, migraine and tenion headache relief, stress reduction, greater sense of well-being, athletic recovery.

How to Float

There are two different types of devices: a pod and a room. The pod is 4 by 8 feet, and an oval shape. It has a lid that closes over it like a clam-shell. The room is bigger. It's a 6.5 by 8.5 feet rectangle (bigger than a king-sized bed!). Both devices function the exact same - they're just different shapes and sizes.

Each device is in a private room with a private shower. The door to the room locks; the door to the device doesn't and you have full control over opening and closing it. You can leave the door ajar if you'd like. The process is super simple: you shower, you float, then you shower again.

Step into the device, sit down, and the moment that you lay back, you'll pop to the surface of the water. You will end up being on the top 3 - 4 inches of the water, and then above the water. You'll want to get a feel for it. Find a hand position that feels comfortable. When you're ready to begin your session, you'll press the buttom with the lightbuld next to it. That will start your timer, slowly turn down the lights, and begin some soft, ambiant music that will play for the first few minutes of the session.

Then its just you and your mind.

My Float Experiences

The environment is just that: an environment. It is space. It's space to think. Space to relax - and truly relax. It's space to parse through the inner workings of your mind, your experience, your past, and how to move forward.

No two floats are the same.

I've had floats where I'll zone out, somewhere in that space between conscious and unconscious. I've also had floats where I'll just sleep for an hour or two and wake up with the music.

I occasionally will caffeinate beforehand and work through ideas, moving into a more creative and productive mode.
